Maharajadhiraja Prithivi Narayan Shah

Founder of Modern Nepal

Here's a time line of Maharajadhiraja Prithivi Narayan Shah's life:

  • 1723 AD - Born in Gorkha
  • 1743 AD
    • Ascended to the throne at the age of 20, after the death of his father King Nara Bhupal Shah.
    • First attempt of invasion at Nuwakot failed.
  • 1744 AD
    • entered into friendly alliance with the chiefs of Lamjung , Tanahun and Palpa.
    • defeated the Nuwakot army.
  • 1756 AD - occupied the Kuti Pass, halting all trade through the pass and preventing communication with Tibet.
  • 1763 AD - conquered Makwanpur, one of the gateways to Nepal from Bengal.
  • 1765 AD - conquered the ancient city Kirtipur on his third attempt.
  • 1768 AD - succeeded in conquering all the cities of Kathmandu valley that still held out.
  • 1774 - 1775 - realizing his end was near after being troubled by illness to a great extent, organized an unofficial council meeting and informed why he was involved with the unification of Nepal and what should the appropriate policies and programs for the future be regarding Nepal. The messages he gave were written down and compiled as Divyopadesh, also Divya Upadesh(Divine Counsel)
  • 1775 AD - At the age of 52, Prithvi Narayan Shah died at Devighat, Nuwakot on his birthday.

"मेरा साना दुखले आर्ज्याको मुलुक होइन यो चार बर्ण छात्तिश जात सबैको साझा फूलबारी हो सबैलाइ चेतना भया ।
(This is not the nation gained by my trifle efforts, this is the garden of all 4 main castes and 36 sub castes, and may all be aware of this.)"
--Maharajadhiraja Prithivi Narayan Shah
